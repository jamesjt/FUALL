# Foundational Understanding — Article Outline

The framework as it currently stands. Each entry = one article or article series.

## Site Structure: Knowledge Tree, Not Blog

The site is not linear articles — it's a knowledge map people can trace ideas along. **Wisdom is the seed.** It branches into **Reality, Reason, Right.** Everything else grows from those three branches. The existing Wisdom Map visualization and reference tooltip system already support this. Each article is a **node in the tree** that must work both as:
1. A standalone read (someone lands here directly)
2. A connected node (clear parent branch, sibling nodes, tangent paths)

Branching, tangents, and depth are features, not problems. A reader exploring Reality should be able to follow that branch deep into metaphysics, epistemology, or science without losing their way back to the trunk. The tree structure means articles can reference each other through tooltips and links — the same way the reference system already works for terms.

## The Core Chain

### 1. Foundations
The opening. Erosion is happening again. Fundamental questions we can't answer. Ignorance as the starting point. Reason as our capacity to find truth. Reality as what we're trying to understand. Right as how we should live. Wisdom = Reality + Reason + Right. The positive feedback loop: knowledge → well-being → more knowledge. This has been discovered and lost multiple times throughout history.

*Sources: Foundations article (published), Page 2 Fundamentals flow, Intro to Philosophy, v1 outline*

### 2. The Erosion Mechanism
Why does the Geist keep fading? Three modes of engaging with truth: Pathos (cheap activation, dominant), Mythos (substantive but imprecise, mostly lost), Logos (precise but aversive). Stories are cognitively cheap; precision is cognitively expensive. The master decay pattern: "lost the referent, kept the symbol." Myths forgot what spirits pointed to. Universities forgot wisdom and kept tenure. Political terms forgot values and kept brands. Same mechanism every time.

*Sources: Mythos/Logos conversation, Sneaky Candy (published), mythos voice note, Poseidon analogy from Page 2*

### 3. Beauty
Not aesthetics. The felt sensation when truth or excellence lands — when an idea rings true, it feels magical, beautiful. Easy to imagine how myth surrounded this as "seeing the face of god." Beauty-as-excellence is what you feel when Logos lands. The myth transmitted that sensation before we had epistemology. The debased version is chasing the feeling without the substance (Pathos).

*Sources: "Excellence is beauty" note, review conversation insights. Needs: classical reading on beauty*

### 4. Power
Four domains: Physical (violence), Resources (money), Social (influence), Ideational (ideas). The ideal: all conflict stays in the ideational domain. The other three serve rather than corrupt it. The cascade: when the ideational domain weakens, conflict drops to social, then resources, then physical. Citizens United as example — resource power overriding ideational. The marketplace of ideas requires boundaries on the other domains, like we created boundaries on physical violence.

*Sources: Domains-of-power voice transcript, Page 2 On Power and Politics, ChatGPT draft outline sections 3-6, reading lists by domain*

### 5. Political Spectrums — Overview
Left/Right isn't meaningless — it's an aggregate of aggregates. The terms have suffered the "lost referent" decay. We confuse Values, Brand, Base Identity, Leader's Speech, and Leader's Actions. Unpacking reveals at least three orthogonal axes, each analyzable via Telos, Tensions, Trials, Tragedies. Plus sub-spectrums as specific applications.

*Sources: Page 2 Politics section, "Aims of Power" draft, Mis-Wired Compass essays, "Rot" passage, left/right uncertainty citations (as evidence of erosion)*

### 5a. Progressive vs Conservative
Risk-orientation spectrum, not partisan identity. Progressivism = confidence in reason, willingness to spend stability on improvement. Conservatism = society's immune system, protecting hard-won gains. Innovation engine vs immune system. The fracturing problem: progressives splinter across problem definition, solution, strategy, cost allocation, timing. Memetic corruption: conservatism blind to internal mutation like immune systems blind to cancer. The Berwick quote: "Not all change is improvement, but all improvement is change."

Position names: Revolutionary → Radical → Reforming → Reticent → Reluctant → Restrained → Resistant → Rooted → Rigid → Resolute.

*Sources: 06a unique content, Mis-Wired Compass essays, Page 2 multiple drafts (merge best elements), Goldratt/driverless cars*

### 5b. Liberal vs Authoritarian
Who should be able to do what. Not just law — culture, custom, Nomos. The credential chain: Ideational (skill) → Social (credential/authority) → Resource (trade restriction) → Physical (enforcement). Democracy depends on a floor of wisdom — without it, populism or technocracy. "We have no mass wisdom program and never have." Liberty dimensions: Violence, Speech, Assembly, Create (IP), Rent Seek, Resources, Land, Income, Substance, Trade, Animal welfare, Transitions of power, Travel.

*Sources: LvA spectrum notes, "Aims of Power" draft, credential chain (from review conversation)*

### 5c. Collectivism vs Individualism
Who are we responsible for, at what social distance? Bronfenbrenner's Ecological Systems Theory as the frame. The three left axes are causally linked through feedback loops: progress creates displacement → individual cost = discontent → instability → progress riskier. Collective cost = smooth path → safer progress. Liberty expands innovator pool. Suppressing discontent via force costs more than paying for it. Collectivism = infrastructure for progress, not charity.

Sub-spectrums: Openness/Borders (CvI), Hedonism/Restraint (LvA+CvI+Ethics — golden mean per ring per era), Forgiving/Damning (CvI), Privacy/Security (LvA).

*Sources: Review conversation insights (largely new), driverless cars example, Page 2 left-values cohesion passage. Needs: novelty research*

### 6. Education — The Transmission Mechanism
Six institutions, each serving a function any civilization needs: Academy (wisdom, citizens), Guilds (skills, workers), Laboratory (discovery), News (current events), Agora (public discourse), Temple (moral synthesis, community). All currently failing. The university abandoned the Academy to become the Guild. Without the Academy, the Temple has no qualified staff and the Agora has no informed participants. Podcasters fill the Temple void without Academy training. Andragogy — this is about adult enlightenment, not children's schooling.

*Sources: Mythos/Logos conversation, 07-education institution notes, Civic Renaissance doc, 3D's of Discourse (published — demonstrates what Academy output looks like)*

### 7. The Mission
Archers. The first-ever mass wisdom program. The Grandfather Principle: teach his grandfather to shoot. We're not building the Academy that produces wise people — we're building the culture that produces the culture that eventually produces wise people. Every year the chain is broken, the gap widens. Success requires three things built together: Skill (Reason) + Spirit (willingness to pursue wisdom) + Worldview (ideology). The university failed by separating these into departments.

*Sources: Mythos/Logos conversation, review conversation, IDENTITY.md*
