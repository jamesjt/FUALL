# Western tradition and progress

*Note distilled from Agora 4/22/26 — The Hermosillo on York, discussion on whether progress is "mostly a Western value."*

## What "Western" actually means

If "Western" is defined as the **Greek → Hellenistic → Roman → Renaissance → Enlightenment** lineage, the claim that progress is mostly a Western value holds up cleanly.

The confusion comes from conflating "Western" with "geographic Europe." The European medieval period (~200–1200 CE) wasn't the West failing — it was the Western tradition being *displaced* in its home geography by institutional Abrahamic religion, then preserved through Byzantium and the Islamic Golden Age, then recovered from Petrarch onward.

The Renaissance and Enlightenment are literal **rebirths** — the West reasserting itself against that interruption.

## Timeline of displacement and recovery

- **180 CE** — Marcus Aurelius dies. End of the integrated Stoic-philosopher-emperor model; last flowering of the Greek-Roman synthesis at the imperial level.
- **313 CE** — Edict of Milan. Christianity legalized.
- **380 CE** — Edict of Thessalonica. Christianity becomes state religion of Rome.
- **391 CE** — Theodosius closes pagan temples; destruction of the Serapeum.
- **529 CE** — Justinian closes the Platonic Academy in Athens. Symbolic end of open Greek philosophy in the Christian Mediterranean.
- **750–1258** — Islamic Golden Age preserves and extends Greek thought, but inside an Abrahamic framework. Eventually constrained too — al-Ghazali's *Incoherence of the Philosophers* (~1095).
- **~1336** — Petrarch climbs Mt. Ventoux; humanist recovery of classical antiquity begins.
- **1440s–1500s** — Renaissance: explicit, self-conscious reach *past* the medieval Christian period to recover Greek and Roman heritage.
- **1500s–1700s** — Scientific Revolution + Enlightenment: pushing back against Church authority to re-enthrone reason.
- **1859** — Darwin. **1882** — Nietzsche declares "God is dead." The recovery is complete; Western progressivism is fully autonomous from Abrahamic constraint.

## What this framing explains

- Why Galileo was persecuted — Abrahamic authority vs. Western method.
- Why the Enlightenment was anti-clerical (Voltaire, Diderot, Hume).
- Why Spinoza was excommunicated.
- Why early modern science mostly happened outside Church institutions.
- Why the U.S. founders cited Greek and Roman precedents, not Christian ones, for the republic's design.
- Why China's "longest technological lead in history" sits comfortably alongside the claim that progress is Western — China was operating in a different civilizational current; it never had the Greek metaphysical-methodological infrastructure that produces *modern* science.

## Adjacent claim worth its own examination

> **Abrahamic religion and the Greek-Western progress tradition are in structural tension.**

Qualifications: Aquinas attempts synthesis; many Enlightenment thinkers remained Christian; monasteries did preserve texts; some traditions within Christianity (especially Protestant rationalism) align more closely with Greek inheritance. But the institutional framework — religious authority asserting truth from revelation rather than reason — has historically constrained the open inquiry that progress requires.

## Implications for "Progress is mostly a Western value"

The claim survives the medieval-gap objection if we hold the lineage definition. The verdict for the claim should make the definitional move explicit, so the claim isn't read as "everything Europeans did is the source of progress" — which is the weaker, harder-to-defend version.
