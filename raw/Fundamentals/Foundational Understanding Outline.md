# Foundational Understanding Outline

In recent years it has become quite clear that the foundations of our society are eroding. Socially we’ve felt a rise in racism, sexism, and tribalism on both sides of our two party system. 
Intellectually we’ve seen the regression of good science, the liberal arts, access to education. In this book I’ll talk about the foundations of modern society (enlightenment values, social contracts, democracy, and capitalism) and how they are falling apart. 

Part 1: What are these things? 
- Enlightenment values/intro to philosophy
- First order questions (why do things exist at all, what is the meaning of life, etc.)
- Accepting ignorance for the pursuit of knowledge
- The search for, value of, and discovery of truth
- What is knowledge?
- Justified true belief - Plato
- Scientific method: Reason and empiricism together 
- Theories of truth, correspondence, coherence, etc (might not be worth including) 
- Societal knowledge vs individual knowledge
- Reliable sources of knowledge and how we decide to trust
- Personal experience, logic, probabilistic reasoning, etc. to confirm trust
- Well-being. We exist, and discovery takes a long time, so we should live well. 
- Living well for ourselves and for creating stable society for continued progress
- Freedom is the core aspect of well-being. Synonymous with opportunity?
- Best word we have to encapsulate what we know about human well-being
- Social contract - the base foundation of cooperation, 
- As groups move beyond the Dunbar number anarchy and unstructured economies no longer function. Our minds are limited and can’t keep track of that many people. So we have formed social contracts culminating in a shared culture (values, behaviors, etc) to help us recognize the familiar and safe from the unfamiliar or unsafe.
- Law is a social contract, which society enforces with legal monopoly on physical force (violence)
- Variance in interests, comforts, and preferences between people. 
- Contract has to stay fairly abstract to allow for this diversity
- The Sorting problem (not sure of this goes here or within another area, like the social contract)
- On Power - Democracy
- In larger groups a hierarchy inevitably develops to create and enforce laws. The leaders are backed by the weight of the institutions of violence (law enforcement and military).
- How do we decide who should have this power?
- Aristocracy, Timocracy, Oligarchy, Democracy, Tyranny from Annarchy 
- Find the best of us to lead, but how do we do that?
- Current belief is that all sorting methods are bad, but representative democracy (trusting in crowd wisdom to elect representatives) is the least bad. 
- Explore political spectrums later or go into that here? 
- Political leaders real actions vs perception (or brand/tribe) vs actual political values (place on a spectrum)
- On Resources - Capitalism
- How should be distribute resources between individuals in society
- What is the right place on the spectrum for spending resources on current vs future well-being.
- The largest remaining grey area of morality. How much should we sacrifice for the future, not just for future generations but for economic (which also means technological) progress without our own lifetime. 
- How do we get more resources/reduce scarcity/become more productive
- Capitalism  - what it actually means
- Workers give up some of the value of their contributions so that there is profit, a return on investment. 
- In return those who are better are reducing scarcity and creating value will be incentivized to do so by letting them keep some of the profit (keeping some of the value that others are creating or getting more than their fair share)
- Products are actually increasing value to society or reducing scarcity. 
- How do we find the best people to create value and reduce scarcity
- Free market, that is augmented to make sure competition happens by preventing too much power from being in one place. Enabling new competitors. Turning things into ‘infrastructure’ once they have been optimized, meaning forcing things to become non-profits? Open sourcing code?
- Why ‘take away the economic freedom of people who made billions’? Just as there are places we take away freedom of power and physical action the extent of economic freedom is hurting many people for the individual. This is part of the social contract, through this contract they already became rich they don’t get to just sit there rent seeking, this is what the contract should be.

Clout is like a social mass. If it gets large enough it has gravity, can be used to push other things around both directly and indirectly (catering to people with clout). 

We’ve taken generally agreed to remove or minimize the physical force from the field of play. That leaves social force and mental force. Social force is influence, is clout. Mental force is creation, is progress of technology and ideas. 

We still give the social force a huge amount of power. It often overpowers the mental force. We see this in the disc all the time. It is bret weinsteins story, he wasn't in a position to publish the result of his mental force because of the social force Carol W. Greider had. Prestige, position, trust, influence. 

VCs largely decide based on social force rather than mental force, as they are largely incapable of deciding based on mental force. They don’t set themselves up to make good investment decisions into fields as they invest in fields they don’t understand and trust a team rather than ideas to win the day. 

Much like physical force prevents progression so does social force. Wwe need to create an agreement to minimize/remove the social forces at play like we did with the physical ones.

We need to remove obstacles to the mental force as much as possible, which includes giving people opportunity to use it rather than keep a huge number of resources in the hands of few granting them significant social force.
