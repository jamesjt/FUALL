# In addition to that goal western culture has sought to live well, to improve

In addition to that goal western culture has sought to live well, to improve well-being. To give people freedom and opportunity to grow, learn, enjoy life, and contribute to progress. A fairly stable society that lasts through time to pass down our discoveries and share the gains in well-being with the future has become a key value.
Progress and stability have a complex relationship. Gaining confidence in a path of progress requires some discovery, which requires resources that could otherwise be spent on maintain stability. Progress can be made in lots of places, political, social, economic. Economic progress can mean development of new technology, improved logistics, or infrastructure. This makes things less scarce or provides new opportunities. 

The conservative side of the spectrum is relatively easy. It’s job is to maintain the stability that we have now, which means being fairly resistant to change. 

The progressive side is very difficult. A progressive is saying they have found something better than what we have now. Or that they think there is a chance  we can have something likely to be better than what we have now and trying  is worth the risk or cost.

However, there is not just one path of progress. Progressives need to prove or persuade conservatives they must do the same with each other progressive path that differs from their own. 

As resources are finite and you can only take so much risk at a time we have to prioritize where we choose to spend resources and take risks for progress. N

The ratio of risk and reward is the primary crux of the PvC spectrum. 

Reasons to be progress on an issue:

You think a change is worth the risk:
-  because what we have now is dysfunctional
-  because the risk is so low and gains very clear
- 

Reasons to be conservative:
- Don’t trust the people asking for the change in power to be better while in it
- Don’t believe the risks are low
- Don’t believe the gains are clear
- Don’t believe what we have now is all that ineffective

One of the problems with progress is the opposition. There is inherent risk to try to progress something. Opposition to this that can meddle with the efficacy of the progress can create a false understanding of that path of progress
