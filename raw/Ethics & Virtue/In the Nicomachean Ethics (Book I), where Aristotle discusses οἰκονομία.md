# In the Nicomachean Ethics (Book I), where Aristotle discusses οἰκονομία

In the Nicomachean Ethics (Book I), where Aristotle discusses οἰκονομία (oikonomia) and its end, the Greek word translated as "wealth" is πλοῦτος (ploutos). This term refers to wealth or resources in a general sense, but in Aristotle's context, it denotes the material means sufficient to achieve self-sufficiency (αὐτάρκεια, autarkeia) and support the good life (εὐδαιμονία, eudaimonia).Contextual Meaning of πλοῦτος:- Sufficiency for Self-Sufficiency: For Aristotle, πλοῦτος in the context of οἰκονομία is not wealth for its own sake or limitless accumulation but the resources needed to maintain a household's independence and meet its needs. This aligns with his idea of autarkeia, where a household or individual has enough to live well without external dependence.- Support for the Good Life: The "good life" (eudaimonia) involves living virtuously and fulfilling one’s potential. Πλοῦτος provides the material foundation (e.g., food, shelter, tools) to free individuals from want, enabling them to pursue ethical and intellectual activities, such as philosophy or political engagement.In Politics (Book I, 1256b-1257a), Aristotle clarifies that πλοῦτος in the natural scope of οἰκονομία is limited to what is necessary for a well-ordered household, contrasting it with chrēmatistikē (wealth-getting for accumulation), which he views as unnatural when unbounded.Summary:The Greek word for "wealth" in the sense of sufficiency for self-sufficiency and supporting the good life is πλοῦτος (ploutos). It represents the resources required for a household to achieve autarkeia and enable eudaimonia, not wealth as an end in itself.
