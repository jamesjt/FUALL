# 🧭 Public Engagement Strategy Building the Civic Renaissance Movement

🧭 Public Engagement Strategy: Building the Civic Renaissance Movement

I. Messaging Pillars

- 
“Rebuild the Commons of Meaning”
→ Use this phrase to frame the failure across all six domains as a single cultural crisis.
→ Reinforce the idea that this isn’t left vs. right, but disintegration vs. reintegration.

- 
“Wisdom is a Public Good”
→ Argue that like roads or clean water, wisdom requires sustained, public cultivation—not just personal effort or private institutions.

- 
“You are a steward of civilization”
→ Empower ordinary citizens, teachers, guildmasters, and community leaders to see themselves as part of something larger.

III. Strategic Actions by Phase
🛠️ Phase 1: Seeding the Idea (Months 1–6)

- 
Launch a dedicated site and visual brand (e.g., civicrenaissance.org)

- 
Publish an animated explainer video (3–5 minutes) introducing the six domains

- 
Host roundtable podcasts or salon discussions on each domain (6-part series)

- 
Publish manifesto excerpts in Plough, American Purpose, The Atlantic, New Atlantis, Quillette

🌱 Phase 2: Local Prototypes (Months 6–18)

- 
Pilot:

- 
A trivium-based “citizen seminar” curriculum

- 
A Guild–Academy hybrid afterschool program

- 
A Civic Forum hosted weekly at a library or community center

- 
A local Temple event (with moral interpreters reviewing the week’s news, science, and values)

- 
Build partnerships with:

- 
A charter network or classical school

- 
A public trade school

- 
A city library system

- 
A podcast or Substack community

🪴 Phase 3: Networked Movement (Years 2–4)

- 
Create a Civic Renaissance Alliance: a distributed network of groups aligned with the six-domain vision

- 
Launch a “Temple & Agora Fund” to pay facilitators, interpreters, and civic elders

- 
Begin building a policy framework to pitch to local and state governments

- 
Host an annual Civic Renaissance Congress

IV. Narratives to Avoid

- 
❌ “Return to tradition” → Feels nostalgic and reactionary

- 
❌ “Fix education” → Too narrow; people will assume it’s a K–12 problem

- 
❌ “Fight misinformation” → Feels partisan, echoes broken News domain logic

✅ Narratives to Use Instead

- 
🗝️ “Reconnect the broken organs of civilization.”

- 
🛠️ “We’re not tweaking the system. We’re re-forging it.”

- 
🕯️ “A society is only as wise as the institutions that cultivate its citizens.”
