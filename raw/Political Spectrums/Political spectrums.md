One might then ask, what do they actually stand for? How do they navigate the complexities of the contemporary world while striving for wisdom and minimizing befuddlement? 

In seeking answers, I’ve found the more accessible writing and models to be disappointing ( Political Compass, Pournelle Chart, Nolan Chart, Political Triangle or just general Left vs Right)  https://kaiserscience.wordpress.com/reading-connections/history/politics/

Intent
 I’ve found various common models for thinking about power to be largely insufficient for my understanding of how to think about my own ideology. Political Compass or Nolan Chart or just general Left vs Right did not help me sufficiently build a world view or even help me think about the nuances of the world view of others. 
Below I strive to accurately categorize what people kind of mean when they say ‘the Left’ or ‘the Right’. Manys issues will require a more concrete resolution than what I present here, However, I think just this resolution creates a much clearer picture of political opinions and brands, and does so adding a lot of depth for only a small increase in complexity.
Different political movements generally include many different issues and can prioritize them differently. Issues generally fit into at least one of these spectrums or where they overlap.

Primary Spectrums

On progress. Not every change is improvement. But every improvement is change

Progressive vs Conservative
Progressive[1] - the more progressive you are, the more you are willing to risk or sacrifice what we have for a chance at a better future, and the more open you are to anything that might mean better.
Conservative - the more conservative you are the more you are unwilling to risk what we have for a chance at a better future, and are more skeptical or resistant of anything claiming to be better. 

Dystopian Ends - Willing to risk all of civilization for small gains vs unwilling to risk anything for large gains
Utopian Ideal - Account for, minimize, and isolate risk even for small gains vs we are at optimal good or the proper path is the natural order (and thus human intervention moves us away from it).

Conservative distopia also relates to needing to much justification for the risk, not being willing to spend resources on the opportunity or even to validate the risk:reward

*The utopian ideal for progressive includes recognizing where risks for different aspects of progress have changed over time. (as we automate more and more UBI becomes a better option for a lot reasons). 

[1] The relationship of truth to progress is, I think, implicit but to state it explicitly: A change in what we have needs to be guided by good reason with a strong understanding of underlying facts. Momentum otherwise can easily lead in the wrong direction. Progress is the path that leads to something better, the hard part is proving any given path is progress. Often that level of proof we can provided can only be derived from reason and not science, you have to take a path before you can collect data on it. 

How ‘radical’ someone is on a side of a progressive vs conservative spectrum can relate to two things. First it can relate how many things they think should be changed and how many can be changed at the same time. Second it can relate to what extreme someone is willing to go through to create or prevent change on a specific topic. We need better words to clearly discuss that difference.  

Liberal vs Authoritarian
Liberal - Personal freedom is to be valued. You have some confidence that the higher parts of human nature will win over the lower. 
 Authoritarian - Expertise is to be valued, as are credentials and respect of hierarchy. 
You have some confidence that the lower parts of human nature will win over the higher. 

Cultures aren’t just one or the other, they generally they create an authoritarian viewpoint of their own beliefs and a liberal one of the opposing tribe’s. Example: No one should be free to have guns but everyone should be free to have an abortion, or vice versa. 

 Dystopian ends: anarchy vs single authority, autocracy
 Utopian Ideal: Communist Anarchy vs Benevolent Philosopher King

Collectivism vs Individualism
Collectivist -  How free the collective is to pull from or harm the individual 
Individualist - How free the individual is to pull from or harm the collective

Dystopian ends  Totalitarian vs very intrusive monarchy
Utopian ideal: Functional Communist State vs Libertarian Utopia (Anarcho-capitalism?)

A resolution down more concrete than this would look at cultural aspects of schema related to locus of control, civil duty, the value of the social contract, and the legal system in regards to the 4th stage of Kohlberg's moral development. 

What should the individual be responsible for? On the right we have just themselves and as we go further left we have greater groups of people. What is the boundary of the collective and of each responsibility is one where the individual is responsible for the collective’s well-being. Mandated taxes that go to the school is an example of an individual who is responsible for the education passed down to all people’s children, not just their own.

Lesser Spectrums
Privacy vs Security
 Privacy -  How much security we should sacrifice for privacy
 Security - How much privacy we should sacrifice for security 

 Dystopian Ends: Can’t find/punish defectors vs thought crime or crime due to perceived intention
Utopian Ideal: Freedom to make mistake or be different vs minimum force and restriction need to make sure no one comes to any significant harm
 
Hedonism/Indulgence vs Restraint (part of liberal vs authoritarian?)
 Hedonism - how much short term pleasure we should seek
 Restraint - how much short term pleasure we should deny

Dystopian ends: no one is  productive as everyone is stuck in self indulgence vs no short term pleasure can exist
Utopian Ideal: A world nearly entirely post scarcity where people are free to indulge as much as they want vs a world where everyone is unhappy in their restraint and its viewed as a noble sacrifice for the future to not indulge

Forgiving vs Damning
 Forgiving - How easily we should forgive
 Damning - How harshly we should punish

Dystopian ends: No wrong should be punished vs small wrongs should result in torture maiming and/or death
 Utopian Ideal: Everyone can make up for mistakes vs we remove all bad actors

Openness vs Borders
 Openness - Welcoming of diversity and pluralism
 Borders - Closed to diversity and pluralism

 Dystopian Ends: Degradation of culture as it can’t be said one is better than another  vs 
constant tension and vigilance toward your neighbor such that resources used significant stifle or flat line progress. 
 Utopian ideal:  Diverse Monoculture vs Ethnic stagnation?

We also likely need something like a Citizen’s Wage as we automate more efficiently. We need to smooth out the problems with Capitalism that create negative feedback loops or flatline its progress.

Gun rights Liberal: yellowish, the dangers of gun rights seem to outweigh the costs to liberty nor the financial and practical costs of significant change

Social Values Liberal: orangeish, we should all things that cause no long term harm and most short term things that don’t greatly cause a dip in societal progress. Two obvious things to not tolerate are intolerance, and less progressive cultures that don’t want to integrate.
