On progressivism

Progress stems from the belief that our current state is not good enough, driving us to explore and innovate for to make things better.
How our current state aren't good enough, and what better looks like, and how to get there are the trials of the progress. 

There’s multiple levels or vectors of alignment that need to happen.

At the top there is the shared goal. What are we trying to achieve in progress? What is not good enough.

Take what Trump and Elon are doing around USAid right now. There’s multiple ways in which USA seems to have failed.

One of those is simply what is the goal of USA there are arguments that are coming out that while the main goal of helping other people other countries is worthy that has been corrupted and co-opted into something other than that.

So it’s failed in being a force for trying to create the better than hoped to be.

Even if it Actions were still in line with its purpose, one question how to make its actions better towards its purpose.

Better can mean more effective more efficient or taking a different strategy or tactics. So effectively the same old strategy, tactics execution aspect as other things I speak of.

Each of these layers can be a personnel problem, a cultural problem, a system structure problem, a technology problem

As such make better could mean inventing, new technology or utilizing technology is not being utilized. It could mean the structure and rules or laws around the thing need to change. It could be that the culture has come become complacent and effective or too conservative when we need progress. Or it could be a personnel problem around leadership that doesn’t know how to effectively or agree upon things needing to be better.

One of the problems with progress is often that you need to tear apart something that is in existence now. But this is only one of the cost of progress.

Sometimes you can just add onto a functional thing to make it more effective.

But often you have to break apart some part of it to rebuild it better. In this case, you have to account for systemic affect of the things you’re taking down the relationships. It has with everything else and account for dependencies synergies, and the like.

Effectively, you have to find out how much baby there is in the bathwater so you can effectively and responsibly tear it down without having to build up from the bottom

Over sometimes that simply just can’t be done sometimes the mess is so inherent and foundational to what has happened or so much of it is corrupt that you have to burn it down and start over.

Which is what might justify seeing with US aid potentially the department of education as Trump and Elon are dismantling them.

There are other costs of progress one is simply the opportunity cost of resources towards other areas of progress or maintaining the system better as it is now.

Another is that progress often means something more efficient and approved or else you wouldn’t be trying to create that progress this often means some sort of cost to the thing that does the job you’re trying to make more effective

Driverless cars would be a huge boon to the world and the cost to the lives of truckers, taxi, drivers, and various other people that work as professional drivers is incredibly significant to their individual life

Another problem of progress is it an immediately fractures or conservatism is inherently skeptical of things that are claiming to be better progress can often fracture not only on where we should spend resources to create progress and which cost we should make to make the progress, but also which progress we shouldn’t move towards or start fixing

Stability is one of those cost of progress. The more you are changing the more you’re often costing short term value to somebody.

If we wanna rework the education system and that’s an agreed-upon thing that is not effective enough there’s a whole bunch of different ideas on how it might happen and that I can quickly fracture into even though there’s a alignment on the goal of making education better how to achieve it

Which can happen at the strategic tactical and execution layer

And then there’s a higher level fracture of the education system might be bad, but it’s not as bad as the medical system so let’s focus on that first and spend our resources accounting for the cost and the development of something better there instead of education

As such creating alignment behind a progressive movement can be difficult as it will quickly fracture based off of priority of problems and solutions and paths to that solution in each different potential priority or project

Someone can also be a progressive in the realm of education in the conservative in a different realm

Someone could believe we should be spending a lot of resources to try to fix education but very conservative on fixing healthcare.

And people can recognize that education is not very effective, but not believe that we have a good path to making it better so it’s not worth touching even if it’s broken because there isn’t a good strategy tactical and practical execution of achieving making it better.

As such this spectrum of progressive versus conservative is incredibly deep and nuanced, and is the most complex of these three spectrums, although all our complex

And as with all political systems by which I mean, systems of power or where power should be held. It all relates to the foundational ideology of a culture.

As that will determine what better even meansp.

As such, we need to find better language to communicate both the area someone believes we need to have progress in their goal with the progress and their strategy for achieving.

Part of this will be how they pay for cost, which will be a relationship with the individuals versus collective of spectrum. For example, a more collectivist culture might believe that the unemployment that comes along for drivers with the invention of driverless cars should be all or park paid for by the collective instead of just places a burden to those individuals, and then the question becomes how to do so, and this should be tied up in the strategy of that area of progress.

The cost of progress can also be kicked down the road or paid upfront. In the case of burning something down because it’s so broken like we’re seeing with USAid the baby in the bathwater is the cost that will be paid by the people that are benefiting from this program. The question becomes for those that want progress is sacrificing the good it’s doing to build something better worth it. In this case their answer was yes, and that cost will be paid by those people that were benefiting from it until something fills that vacuum of need.

However, you can you can also mortgage the future instead of sacrifice the present

I prefer to live in a world in which the future is sacred, and we should be sacrificing the present to build the better future
