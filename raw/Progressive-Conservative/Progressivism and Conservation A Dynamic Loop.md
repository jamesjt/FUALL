# Progressivism and Conservation A Dynamic Loop

Progressivism and Conservation: A Dynamic Loop

As we build we discover practices that advance our goals; once proven useful, those practices become cultural assets we preserve.

Goals here means working toward the “Prime Project” of improving our understanding of Reality, Reason, and Right.

We do not yet possess perfect knowledge of existence, optimal social design, or infallible methods of finding truth, so we improve.

So we explore new ideas, access their efficacy, throw out what doesn't work, integrate and preserve what does, all while continuing to explore new ideas. 

Overtime we grow the amount of ideas that we've found utility in, things we've come to speak about in terms like 'traditional values',  'sacred beliefs', etc. 

However, this can go wrong a variety of ways:
- Memetic mutation
- Changing circumstance
- Corrupt leaders
- Unwise commonsMemes

Dawkins’ meme is both the core concept that illustrates the problem and the perfect use case of how memes mutate and potentially degrade.

Memes, like genes, carry information, self-replicate, mutate, and respond to selective pressures. Their selective pressures while much faster than genes are still in the order of years, generations, individual lifetimes, or civilizational lifetimes. 

However, as they mutate they seem to be more vulnerable to degradation than genes are, or at least the other aspects of biology that I don't understand that might try to prevent mutation that creates less viable organizations isn't sufficiently in place yet for memes. 

As such we use terms to try and convey concepts to pass down ideology and maintain values and stability in society we often see those terms start to become related to different, even if related concepts. Nuance, the relationship of one meme to another, or depth of the meme gets lost. 

The ease of transmission of a meme is often at odds with the complexity or depth of it. As perfectly seen with the term meme itself. 

As I'm writing this if I google memes, Dawkins' meme doesn't show up at all on the first page of google results. If I google meme, internet meme on wikipedia is the first result - Dawkins' meme is the 4th also wikipedia. when asking grok 'what is a meme' this is the response:
A meme is a piece of media—often an image, video, or phrase—that spreads rapidly online, typically with humorous or satirical intent. It evolves through user adaptations, reflecting cultural trends or shared experiences. Think of it as a digital inside joke that mutates as it circulates.We need a strong feedback cycle between progressivism and conservatism to build effective culture. Dawkins' meme was progress, and important term and concept in understanding culture, and conservatism failed to protect the concept and term and it has already mutated in a matter of decades, a couple generations, into a term that represents  a concept of very little value relative to its original concept. 

Simplification for transmission is a huge problem with memes. It is something that even with our most important ones happens often, and it becomes incredibly important to pay attention to this mutation and work to make sure only good mutations happen. Words incredibly important to societal well-being mutate and we must be on guard or we lose the soul of our society. 

And much like our immune system is bad at detecting cancer conservatism is bad at detecting memetic mutation, often putting itself in a position of defending a meme that it doesn't notice mutated and lost its utility or much of it in doing so. 

Mutation sources:

Interaction with rival memes (requiring syntheses that may degrade meaning).

Degradation simply through transmission.

Communication and, especially, speaking and writing are our best tools for transferring concepts between minds, yet grammar and syntax are still inadequate for today’s information load and nuance.- Loss of nuance during mutation is significant; what made an idea valuable can be stripped away.Communication Limits and Idea “Corruption”- Writers constantly trade off depth vs. efficiency; over time, cultures favor ease of transmission, sacrificing precision.
- Result: memes grow “lighter” but less sophisticated, a cultural cancer that degrades wisdom faster than we refine it.
- Historically, genuine memetic progress is rare; regression is common.
Conservatism as Cultural Immune System—and Its Blind Spot- Treat conservatism as the immune system guarding core institutions against reckless change and unnecessary cost.
- Sadly, this immune system is blind to the memetic cancer described above; it preserves institutions now riddled with degraded ideas.
- Therefore, genuine progress often feels radical: it must “burn out” corrupted elements that seem sacred to conservatives.
Free Speech: Our Main Anti-Cancer Treatment- Continuous, open self-examination of all ideas—including those labeled “sacred”—is our best defense.
- Authority can thwart this when:
- Livelihoods depend on existing (cancerous) structures.
- Gatekeepers earned positions by hoop-jumping, not philosophical rigor.
- Sheer scarcity of expert time leads to silencing critics (the “tyranny of the majority” flagged in On Liberty).
6. Technology-Driven Solutions- At root, we are “bad at building systems that allow complex, nuanced memes to transmit easily.”
- Better grammar-syntax technology could:
- Deliver digestible summaries and deep dives side-by-side.
- Free experts from endless rebuttals to quackery by raising baseline understanding.
- Examples & aspirations:
- Matt Taibbi’s annotated timelines.
- Interactive “timelines of ideas” that expose the lineage, edits, notes, and sources of every claim with a click.
7. Shared Responsibilities- Conservatism must guard core ideology—the “sacred structure”—against reckless costs.
- Progressivism must keep insisting “what we have is insufficient,” and root out hidden cancers conservatism misses.
- Free speech empowers progress to challenge authority efficiently—provided experts improve meme transmissibility.
- The more clearly everyone grasps complex ideas, the better we all filter valid progressive critiques past the conservative immune system.
- This duty lies on all citizens, especially because cancer typically originates with those who currently hold power.
8. Overlap of Terms in ZZY’s Framework- Note the overlap between “the Right” (as justice/ethics) and “the right” (as political conservatism).
- Future work: assign ZZY-specific labels for each of the three fundamentals:
- Order – structure of the universe.
- Reason/Enlightenment – collective wisdom.
- Right/Justice – ethical correctness.
Summary in One SentenceTo keep society healthy, we must improve the way complex ideas travel—so progress can expose and excise memetic cancers that conservatism, our cultural immune system, is structurally unable to see.
