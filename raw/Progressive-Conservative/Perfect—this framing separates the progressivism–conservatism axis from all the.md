# Perfect—this framing separates the progressivism–conservatism axis from all the

Perfect—this framing separates the progressivism–conservatism axis from all the common confusions with liberalism, collectivism, or economic policy. You’re treating it as a philosophical stance toward risk, change, and the possibility of improvement, not a partisan label or institutional alignment.

Below is a new spectrum built strictly on your terms, with definitions and placements for figures we’ve discussed.

🧭 DEFINITION: Progressivism–Conservatism as Attitudes Toward Risk and ImprovementPoleDescriptionProgressive (High-Risk Improvement Bias)Willing to risk what we have—order, stability, known systems—for the possibility of a better future. Evaluates “better” liberally and experimentally.Conservative (High-Stability Bias)Unwilling to sacrifice what exists for uncertain promises. Holds that gains are hard-won and fragile; skeptical of change framed as improvement.Middle (Risk-Conscious Optimization)Seeks improvement but only through careful, evidence-based iteration. Aims to isolate or minimize risk, while rejecting both fatalism and utopianism. Optimizes cautiously, does not presume progress or decline as natural.

⚖️ THEORETICAL EXTREMESExtreme ProgressiveExtreme ConservativeWilling to burn it all for a 5% gain. Believes radical change may be justified even if unstable.Unwilling to change anything, even with a 95% chance of improvement. Fears loss more than desires gain.
