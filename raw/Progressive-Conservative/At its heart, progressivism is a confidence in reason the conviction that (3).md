# At its heart, progressivism is a confidence in reason the conviction that (3)

At its heart, progressivism is a confidence in reason: the conviction that systematic inquiry into the nature of reality yields insights that make life better, and that advancements can create additional resources—time, talent, technology—to push inquiry, and thus, well-being, still further. This reciprocal dynamic forms a positive feedback loop: deeper knowledge guides smarter interventions; healthier, more secure populations can invest yet more in discovery. Over time, such compounding progress aspires to restore—or newly achieve—a “golden age” marked by widespread enlightenment, where understanding and prosperity reinforce one another in an upward spiral.
