# Primary Spectrums

On progress. Not every change is an improvement. But every improvement is a change
Progressive vs Conservative
Progressive[1] - the more progressive you are, the more you are willing to risk or sacrifice what we have for a chance at a better future, and the more open you are to anything that might mean better.
Conservative - the more conservative you are the more you are unwilling to risk what we have for a chance at a better future, and are more skeptical or resistant of anything claiming to be better. 
Dystopian Ends - Willing to risk all of civilization for small gains vs unwilling to risk anything for large gains
Utopian Ideal - Account for, minimize, and isolate risk even for small gains vs we are at optimal good or the proper path is the natural order (and thus human intervention moves us away from it).Conservative distopia also relates to needing to much justification for the risk, not being willing to spend resources on the opportunity or even to validate the risk:reward
*The utopian ideal for progressive includes recognizing where risks for different aspects of progress have changed over time. (as we automate more and more UBI becomes a better option for a lot reasons). 
[1] The relationship of truth to progress is, I think, implicit but to state it explicitly: A change in what we have needs to be guided by good reason with a strong understanding of underlying facts. Momentum otherwise can easily lead in the wrong direction. Progress is the path that leads to something better, the hard part is proving any given path is progress. Often that level of proof we can provided can only be derived from reason and not science, you have to take a path before you can collect data on it. 
How ‘radical’ someone is on a side of a progressive vs conservative spectrum can relate to two things. First it can relate how many things they think should be changed and how many can be changed at the same time. Second it can relate to what extreme someone is willing to go through to create or prevent change on a specific topic. We need better words to clearly discuss that difference.
