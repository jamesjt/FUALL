Progressive vs Conservative is a type of spectrum, it really isn't even that, it is a multi-dimensional matrix represented as a spectrum on a per issue basis. 

Imagining and speaking about it this way is a shorthand to allow us to communicate more effectively. 
It represents the amount of change. The far left side represents extreme change that we could call revolutionary progress, what ever issue we are trying to revolutionize will want to keep none or very little what it is doing currently. While the far right side of the spectrum wants to resolutely preserve the thing at issue believing it so important to our society's well-being that change it will lead to harm. 

“Progressive vs. Conservative” is actually one-dimensional, none of these  are, but imaging them as a spectrum is a shorthand projection of a multi-dimensional matrix across a huge number of issues. 

We use the spectrum metaphor because it speeds up conversation by collapsing many factors into a single measure of readiness for change. At the far progressive end lie revolutionary proposals that would replace most or all of the current arrangement. At the far conservative end lies resolute preservation—the conviction that altering the status quo would jeopardize society’s well-being.
