# We use the term Politics to mean a variety of different concepts, office or

We use the term Politics to mean a variety of different concepts, office or other power struggles, a synonym of ideology, a field of study, strategy for winning elections, various others, and governance. 

Governance and how we make decisions around it is what I'll mean by politics. 'Left vs Right' is one common way we speak broadly about beliefs and preferences in governance.
Recently, many have dismissed the terms Left vs. Right as useless, meaningless, or nonsense —and while they are currently confusing various people — Left vs Right is not meaningless; it’s an aggregate of aggregates and therefore extremely full of meaning.
Unfortunately, public discourse around these terms has grown increasingly muddled, in part because politicians use them as brands and the public uses them as tribal identity instead of a representations of coherent values. 
Aggregates are incredibly useful for communication when there is good understanding of what they are combining and we have sufficient context to understand what aspect of it we are addressing. We use them all the time to discuss things like 'the West' or Christianity.  values that we have spent thousands of years trying to build society on top of is being lost, again. 

Although the Left vs Right arose from arbitrary seating arrangements, they have since accumulated substantial significance. Each cluster of ideas has internal coherence, yet society often stumbles when we start mixing and matching them across the underlying core spectra:
Left                  vs       Right
Progressive      vs       Conservative
Liberal              vs       Authoritarian
Collectivist        vs       Individualist
Now we often use each these terms to relate to each of these different concepts:

• Values (ideology)
• Party Brand (current public perception)
• Party Base Identity (current beliefs of the social group claiming the party)
• Cult, the people whose identity is tied with the term but aren't really associated with the values.
• Leader's speech (what the party or movement leaders say)
• Leader's actions (what the leaders actually do)
It is the values that we need to make sure don't get away from us. They are what should be holding our selves, parties, movements, and leaders accountable to. 

If we lose sight and understanding of values we lose everything we have been working toward for thousands of years, which has multiple times happened before. 
I've found it useful to break down the underlying spectra into at least these four aspects:

 • Telos – its purpose or utility 
 • Tensions – conflicts with the opposing end or with other spectra.
 • Trials – practical challenges it must overcome.
 • Tragedies – major pitfalls it must avoid.
 • Tradition – the history of the group of beliefs
