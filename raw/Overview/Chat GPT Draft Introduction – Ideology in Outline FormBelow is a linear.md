1 . Philosophical GroundworkReality, Reason, Right

- Reality – Metaphysics: What actually exists and how it hangs together
- Reason – Logic & Epistemology: How we discover truth and correct error.
- Right – Ethics: How we ought to act once truth is in view.
Note: Later sections will show that any complete worldview must balance all three; privileging one distorts the others.

2 . Inherent Tensions- Reality may set hard limits that Reason or ethical ideals try to ignore
- Reason can undermine traditional notions of Right (e.g., science vs. custom).
- Right sometimes demands actions Reality currently renders impossible.
Sorting these frictions inevitably moves us into the realm of politics—i.e., power.
3 . Power as Sorting Mechanism- Definition: Politics decides whose interpretation of Reality, Reason, and Right prevails.
- Power therefore becomes the arbiter when philosophical argument alone cannot settle disagreement.

5 . Why the Ideal Breaks Down (Sketch of Current Problems)- Power Temptation – Leaders cling to violence, money, or prestige when argument fails; entrenched organisations “smother innovation.”
- Marketplace Distortion – Deep pockets underprice rivals, wield IP aw, or raise entry costs until merit loses to capital.
- Lineage Failure – Even wise rulers (Marcus Aurelius) can hand power to the unfit (Commodus) because institutions, not individuals, should handle succession.

6 . Root Causes of FailureA. Education Collapse
- Citizens can’t judge arguments if schools no longer teach logic, rhetoric, and craft.
- Culture is downstream of education; when the former degrades, the whole system ossifies.
B. Economic Capture
- Capitalism without fair entry becomes plutocracy; competition shifts from “better product” to “bigger war-chest.”
- Resource power eclipses Intellectual power

C. Governance Drift
- Democracy presumes an educated demos; without it, populism or technocracy fills the void.
- Regulatory capture lets incumbents codify barriers (e.g., opaque finance, complex IP).

7 . Cultural Responsibility- A liberal society assumes citizens will keep revitalising both democracy and capitalism through critical thought and entrepreneurial craftsmanship.
- When we abdicate that responsibility, the failure is cultural, not merely institutional.

8 . Forward Path (to be elaborated later)- Rebuild educational foundations so every person can enter the Intellectual arena.
- Reform economic rules to reopen genuine competition of ideas and products.
- Restructure governance to prevent any power domain from hijacking the others.
- Cultivate a culture that treats the pursuit of wisdom as its highest status good.
